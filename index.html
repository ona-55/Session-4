Question 4
📞 Calls
A new team member joins an existing reservations project halfway through. What must happen before they begin evaluating calls?
The Answer
✓
They must be fully briefed on the client criteria, evaluation guide and call standards for that specific project
✓
They must review the same resources as the original evaluator — so their scoring is consistent with work already completed
✓
Also accept: review sample evaluations already submitted on the project
Why It Matters
A new evaluator joining without a proper briefing creates inconsistency in the data. The client's results become incomparable across the team.

Question 7
📞 Calls
An evaluator submits a report stating a team member was unfriendly. The client disputes the score. What is missing — and why is this a problem?
The Answer
✓
Specific evidence is missing — what was said, what was not said, and how it relates to the agreed criteria
✓
Without evidence, the observation is an opinion — it cannot be verified or defended
✓
The client cannot act on the finding and IFH cannot support the score
Why It Matters
Every observation in an IFH report must be specific and factual. A score without evidence is a liability for the evaluator, for IFH and for the client relationship.
Award Points — Q7
Round 3 · Q8 Reveal
Question 8
🍽️ Dining
A shopper's report is rejected. They followed the scenario but did not check the venue's website or social pages beforehand. What went wrong?
The Answer
✓
The shopper did not complete all required preparation steps before the visit
✓
Checking the venue's website or social pages is a mandatory shopper responsibility — it ensures they understand the offering and style before arriving
✓
Arriving without this knowledge affects the quality of observations and the credibility of the report
Why It Matters
Preparation is not optional. A shopper who does not research the venue cannot evaluate it objectively — and the report reflects that gap.

The Answer
